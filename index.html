About Me

Biostatistics researcher focused on advancing computational methods for biological data

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Scientist in the Department of Biostatistics at Columbia University, working with Prof. Wenpin Hou. I completed my Ph.D. in Biostatistics and Health Data Science at Duke-NUS Medical School, National University of Singapore in 2024, advised by Prof. Jin Liu.

My research bridges statistics, machine learning, and computational biology. I develop innovative methods for single-cell RNA sequencing and spatial transcriptomics data, with particular expertise in RNA velocity analysis, deep generative modeling, and representation learning. My work aims to uncover the dynamic processes underlying cellular development and disease progression.

Before my doctoral studies, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from Xi'an Jiaotong University in 2019, where I developed my foundation in statistical theory and computational methods.
